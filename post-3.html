How to Register Your Pet's Microchip Easily

A step-by-step guide to securing your pet's identity in the Trackyn database.

One of the biggest misconceptions in pet safety is that getting the microchip implanted is the final step. In reality, a microchip without a registered profile is essentially a blank ID tag. To ensure your pet can be traced back to you, you must register the chip number to a secure database.

Step 1: Locate Your Pet's Microchip Number

Your pet's microchip number is usually 15 digits long. You can find this number on the paperwork provided by your vet or shelter when the chip was implanted. If you have lost the paperwork, simply take your pet to any local vet clinic or shelter and ask them to do a quick scan.

Step 2: Create Your Trackyn Profile

Once you have the number, head over to the Trackyn registration portal. You will need to create an account using your primary email address. This account will act as the master control center for all of your pets.

"Pro tip: Always use a personal email address rather than a work email, as work emails can change if you switch jobs."

Step 3: Enter Your Pet's Details

Add a new pet to your profile. You will be prompted to enter their name, breed, age, and any specific medical conditions a finder might need to know about. Finally, input the microchip number.

Don't forget to keep it updated! If you move houses or change your phone number, logging into Trackyn to update your details should be at the top of your to-do list. A registered chip is your pet's ticket home—make sure the destination is accurate!
